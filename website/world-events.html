● GEX Regime

Gamma Exposure regime. Positive / Dampening = market makers absorb volatility, moves tend to be smaller and mean-revert. Negative / Amplifying = market makers add fuel to moves — up trends accelerate up, down trends accelerate down. Think of it as: Positive = shock absorber, Negative = turbocharger.

📈 Market Direction

The ML model's current overall read on equity market sentiment. Bullish = majority of signals pointing up. Bearish = majority pointing down. Neutral = mixed signals, no clear edge. This is derived from analyzing put/call ratios, GEX levels, and price momentum across 42+ assets.

📑 Bull % / Bear %

Of all assets tracked, what percentage show bullish signals (price above key levels, call flow dominant) vs bearish signals (price below key levels, put flow dominant). Example: Bull 50%, Bear 19% means most assets are bullish, few are bearish — a healthy bull market.

📈 VIX Estimate

VIX is the market's fear gauge — it measures how much options traders expect the S&P 500 to move over the next 30 days. VIX below 15 = calm, complacent market → sell premium strategies work. VIX 15–25 = normal uncertainty. VIX above 25 = fear elevated → buy protection, reduce risk. VIX above 35 = panic — historically a contrarian buy signal.

📊 GEX Dampening vs Amplifying

Dampening (Positive GEX): When SPY goes up, dealers sell stock to re-hedge; when it goes down, they buy. This creates a natural "rubber band" pulling prices back to center — low realized volatility.
Amplifying (Negative GEX): Dealers must buy when price rises and sell when it falls. This feeds momentum — moves tend to be larger and sustained. Breakouts are more reliable.

🔎 GEX Key Levels

Call Wall = strong resistance — heavy call open interest pins price or creates ceiling.
Put Wall = strong support — heavy put open interest creates floor.
GEX Flip Level = the price where dealers shift from net-long to net-short gamma. Above = dampening. Below = amplifying. Acts like a gravitational pull on price.
