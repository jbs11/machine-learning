🔧 18-Variable Feature Vector

Raw OHLCV data is transformed into 18 predictive features across four categories:

Trend Features

  • SMA(5), SMA(20), SMA(50) — Short, medium, and long-term trend direction. Price relative to each SMA signals momentum regime.
  • Price/SMA(20) ratio — How far price has deviated from its 20-day mean. Values below 0.97 suggest mean-reversion opportunity.

Momentum Features

  • RSI-14 — Relative Strength Index. Today's AAPL at 42.3 is near oversold territory — historically a bullish mean-reversion signal. Model weight: highest of all features.
  • MACD crossover — EMA(12) minus EMA(26). A positive crossover at +0.48 confirms upward momentum shift.

Volatility Features

  • Bollinger Band percentile — Where price sits within the upper/lower bands. At 18%, price is near the lower band — classical oversold signal.
  • ATR-14 — Average True Range. At $2.10, this is the daily volatility measure used for stop-loss placement and position sizing.

Volume & Gap Features

  • Volume ratio — Today's volume divided by 20-day average. 1.83× means elevated institutional activity — confirms directional signal.
  • Overnight gap % — Today's open vs. yesterday's close. Small gaps are neutral; large gaps often revert intraday.

🎯 XGBoost Direction Classifier

XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting) is the workhorse of ML stock trading. It builds an ensemble of decision trees, each correcting the errors of the previous, producing a calibrated probability score between 0 and 1.

📐 Magnitude Regression

Direction prediction answers which way. Magnitude regression answers how much. Combined, they enable proportional position sizing — your largest bets when the model is most confident of the largest moves.

Today's Magnitude Output

Predicted return = +1.4% [90th pct interval: +0.2% to +2.8%]
Dollar move at $185.20 entry = +$2.59 per share

Position Sizing Matrix

  • Predicted magnitude < 0.5% → Reduce position to 0.5× standard size — low expected value
  • Predicted magnitude 0.5–1.5% → Standard position size — today's +1.4% falls here
  • Predicted magnitude > 1.5% → Increase to 1.5× standard size — high expected value

⚡ Reading Signals & Placing the Trade

Signal Decision Rules

  • P(UP) ≥ 0.65 AND predicted magnitude ≥ 0.8% → STRONG BUY. Enter tomorrow's open.
  • P(UP) 0.55–0.65 AND magnitude ≥ 0.5% → BUY. Reduced position size.
  • P(UP) 0.45–0.55 → NO TRADE. Insufficient edge.
  • P(UP) < 0.45 AND magnitude ≥ 0.5% → SHORT or buy puts.

Bracket Order Setup

Always enter trades as bracket orders: a single parent order with two child orders attached — a take-profit limit and a stop-loss market. This ensures both exit orders are live the moment you enter. Never rely on manually watching the position.

📏 ATR Position Sizing + Kelly Criterion

ATR Method

Stop distance = 1.5 × ATR-14 = 1.5 × $2.10 = $3.15
Position size = Account risk $ ÷ Stop distance = $100 ÷ $3.15 = 31 shares

For a $10,000 account risking 1% per trade ($100), this gives 31 shares at $185.20 = $5,741 deployed.

Kelly Criterion

f* = (p·b − q) / b = (0.631 × 1.4 − 0.369) / 1.4 = 26%

Full Kelly suggests 26% of account per trade. We use half-Kelly (13%) to reduce variance. At $185.20 per share, 13% of $10,000 = $1,300, consistent with our 31-share position.

Rule: Never risk more than 1–2% of account on a single trade regardless of Kelly output. Kelly is a long-run optimum; real markets have model errors that can occur in clusters.

⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

1. Overfitting

A model trained exclusively on 2022 data will likely underperform on 2024 data as market regimes, volatility levels, and sector correlations shift. Fix: Walk-forward validate only. Never tune parameters on the same data used to evaluate performance.

2. Look-Ahead Bias

RSI at time T must be computed using only prices available before the close of day T. Using today's close to compute today's features — then acting on those features at today's close — is look-ahead bias that cannot be replicated in live trading. Fix: All features use prior session's close data.

3. Transaction Costs

At 187 trades/year with a $0.05 average bid-ask spread: 187 × 31 shares × $0.05 = $290 in spread costs plus commissions. On a $10,000 account generating $3,420 gross return, this represents ~8.5% of gross profit. Fix: Model costs explicitly in backtesting. Only deploy strategies with positive net alpha.
