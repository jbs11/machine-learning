Gamma Exposure Analysis
2.1

Understanding Gamma Exposure (GEX)

What is Gamma Exposure?

Gamma Exposure (GEX) measures the total rate of change of options dealers’ delta hedging activity. When dealers are net short gamma (negative GEX), they amplify moves — buying into strength, selling into weakness. Positive GEX acts as a gravitational magnet, pulling price toward the highest-gamma strike.

🟩 Gamma Wall

Strike with highest net positive gamma. Acts as resistance — dealers sell into strength here, limiting upside.

🔴 Put Wall

Strike with concentrated put OI. Acts as support — dealers buy the underlying here to hedge short puts.

⚡ Flip Level

Price where net GEX crosses zero. Below this, dealers amplify moves (negative gamma regime).

📊 PCR (Put/Call Ratio)

Put OI divided by call OI. Above 1.0 = bearish hedging. Below 0.7 = bullish sentiment, potential complacency.

💰 Total GEX $M

Sum of all strike-level gamma x OI x 100 x spot2. Positive = dealer long gamma (dampening). Negative = amplifying.

🎯 Regime

Positive: price above flip, dealers absorb volatility. Negative: price below flip, dealers chase — expect larger moves.

Options Flow Methodology

How We Read Options Flow

Options flow analysis tracks the real-money activity of institutional traders. Large premium transactions, especially in short-dated expirations, often precede significant price moves. We analyze net premium, dealer delta, and unusual flow patterns to identify directional bias.

💰 Net Premium

Call premium minus put premium. Positive = net bullish money flow. Negative = bears paying for downside protection.

🏠 Dealer Delta

Net delta of market makers. Positive dealer delta means they must sell stock to hedge — creating selling pressure.

🚨 Unusual Flow

Transactions with volume well above average OI, or premium above $1M. Signals informed positioning ahead of catalysts.

📌 Max Pain

Strike where maximum options expire worthless. Market makers have natural incentive to push price here near expiration.

5.3

ML Signal Interpretation Guide

🔵 prob_up > 0.65

Strong bullish signal. High confidence in upward move. Consider long calls, bull spreads, or long stock.

🔴 prob_up < 0.35

Strong bearish signal. Consider long puts, bear spreads, or short stock with defined risk.

🟡 0.35 – 0.65

Neutral zone. No directional edge. Consider iron condors, butterflies, or staying on the sidelines.

📈 Direction: UP

LSTM sequence model predicts upward price movement for next session. Combine with GEX regime for confluence.

📉 Direction: DOWN

Model predicts downward move. Higher conviction when GEX regime is also negative (amplifies drops).

⚡ Magnitude

Predicted % move. Large magnitude + high prob = high conviction trade. Small magnitude = scalp or skip.

🔬 Features Used

RSI, MACD, ATR, Bollinger Bands, 5/20-day momentum, volume trend, and historical return windows.

⚠ Model Limitations

Macro events, earnings surprises, and geopolitical shocks override technical signals. Past performance is not a guarantee.
