How Momentum vs Reversal Setups Are Identified

The classifier combines four signals to determine whether a name is in a Momentum setup (dealer flows amplify moves — follow the trend) or a Reversal setup (dealer flows dampen moves — fade extremes):

① GEX Regime
Short Gamma → momentum · Long Gamma → reversal · Distance from flip level = confidence
② Net Dealer Delta
Negative dealer delta (short shares) = structural buyer on dips → reversal support · Positive = overhead supply
③ Put/Call Ratio
PCR > 1.2 (fear extreme) → reversal upside · PCR < 0.7 (complacency) → momentum exhaustion risk
④ Unusual Flow
Large call sweep + Short Gamma → momentum ignition · Large put sweep + Long Gamma → reversal catalyst

5 Hedging Impact Algorithm — How It Works — Mathematical construction, signal logic, and trading application

What Is Hedging Impact?

Hedging Impact (HI) is an estimate of the net share buying or selling pressure that options market makers and dealers generate through their mandatory delta-hedging activity. Unlike sentiment indicators, this is mechanical, price-agnostic flow — dealers must hedge regardless of their directional opinion.

SpotGamma pioneered this framework commercially. Our implementation reconstructs the core logic from publicly available GEX and options flow data via a four-component model:

HI = Δ-Flow + Γ-Scalp + Charm-Flow + Vanna-Flow
Positive = Net dealer buying · Negative = Net dealer selling · Magnitude = estimated $ flow
Step 1 — Gamma Exposure Profile
Compute GEX at Each Strike
For each options strike, compute the net gamma exposure that dealers have accumulated from their market-making activity:
GEX(K) = Γ(K) × OI(K) × Spot × 100 × (-1 if put) Where: Γ(K) = Black-Scholes gamma at strike K OI(K) = Net open interest (calls - puts, from dealer perspective) Spot = Current underlying price 100 = Shares per contract multiplier -1 = Flips puts (dealers are long puts → short gamma)
Step 2 — Delta Hedge Requirement
Compute Required Dealer Share Position
At each price level P, compute the total shares dealers must hold to remain delta-neutral across all open options positions:
DealerDelta(P) = Σ_K [ Δ(K,P) × OI(K) × 100 × direction ] direction = +1 for calls sold to clients (dealer short call → long Δ) direction = -1 for puts sold to clients (dealer short put → short Δ) HI at price P = DealerDelta(P) - DealerDelta(P_prev) = estimated shares bought(+) or sold(-)
Step 3 — Charm Flow
Time-Decay Delta Migration
Each day, option deltas migrate toward their expiration values (0 for OTM, ±1 for ITM) even without price movement. Dealers must unwind hedges accordingly:
CharmFlow = Σ_K [ charm(K) × OI(K) × 100 × daysToExpiry ] charm(K) = dΔ/dt = -N'(d1) × (r/S×σ×√T - d2/(2T)) Net effect: OTM calls near expiry → charm negative → delta falls → dealers SELL OTM puts near expiry → charm positive → delta rises toward 0 → dealers BUY
Step 4 — Vanna Flow
IV-Driven Delta Changes
When implied volatility changes, all option deltas shift. Vanna captures this sensitivity:
VannaFlow = Σ_K [ vanna(K) × OI(K) × 100 × ΔIV ] vanna(K) = dΔ/dσ = -N'(d1) × d2 / σ ΔIV = change in implied vol since open (from VIX proxy) Effect: Rising IV → puts go deeper ITM → put deltas more negative → Dealers must SELL more shares to hedge rising put delta
Step 5 — Momentum vs Reversal Classifier
Combining Signals Into a Trade Setup
The classifier computes a composite score for each name using four weighted inputs:
MomentumScore = w1×ShortGamma + w2×TrendingNetDelta + w3×UnusualCallFlow + w4×AboveFlipLevel (weights: 0.35, 0.25, 0.25, 0.15) ReversalScore = w1×LongGamma + w2×StretchedPCR + w3×NegativeDealerDelta + w4×AtGammaWall (weights: 0.35, 0.25, 0.25, 0.15) Setup = Momentum if MomentumScore > 60 Reversal if ReversalScore > 60 Neutral otherwise
Momentum signals to act on: Short Gamma + price below flip + unusual call sweeps → ride the trend, use trailing stops, target gamma wall as resistance.

Reversal signals to act on: Long Gamma + price at gamma wall + PCR > 1.1 → fade the move, buy puts at wall, target flip level as support.

⚡ Trading Rules for Hedging Impact

  • Follow the HI direction: Positive HI (dealer buying) = structural bid. Enter longs with dealer flow behind you, not against you.
  • HI extremes = exhaustion warning: When the HI gauge is pegged (90%+ buy or sell), the mechanical flow is near maximum. Expect a fade when the extreme reverses.
  • Charm flow creates daily drift: On expiration days (especially monthly OPEX), charm causes OTM deltas to collapse. Dealers sell hedges all morning → creates predictable AM weakness.
  • Vanna rally setups: After a VIX spike, put IV elevated → as VIX falls, vanna creates massive systematic buying. The "vanna rally" post-VIX-spike is one of the most reliable institutional flows.
  • Short Gamma + momentum = ride the trend: In Short Gamma, dealers amplify every move. Enter in the trend direction at the open and hold with a stop at the gamma flip level.
  • Long Gamma + at gamma wall = highest-probability reversal: The gamma wall is where dealer selling is maximum. Fade rallies into the wall with tight stops above it.
  • Negative dealer delta = structural support: If dealers are net short shares (negative net dealer delta), they MUST buy as price dips — creating a mechanical floor.
  • Monitor PCR velocity: Rapid PCR increase (put buying acceleration) signals institutional hedging stress even before GEX flips negative. Front-run the gamma flip.
