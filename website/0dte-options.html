📘 Understanding 0DTE Options
🔥
What is 0DTE?
Zero days to expiration (0DTE) options are contracts that expire the same day they are traded. By market close, they are either worth their intrinsic value (if in-the-money) or expire completely worthless. For the buyer, it's a high-leverage, all-or-nothing bet on intraday price direction. For the seller (usually a market maker), it's a premium collection with the highest possible gamma risk. Since 2022, 0DTE options have grown to represent over 50% of daily S&P 500 options volume.
⚡
The Gamma Bomb
Gamma measures how fast delta changes as the underlying price moves. For 0DTE options, gamma is at its maximum — especially near ATM strikes. A $1 move in the stock can change an ATM 0DTE delta from 0.50 to nearly 1.00 (or 0.00). This forces market makers to buy or sell hundreds of thousands of shares of stock per contract just to stay delta-neutral. Near expiry, the slightest price move near a high-OI 0DTE strike triggers a gamma feedback loop — up-moves accelerate, down-moves cascade.
📍
Pin Risk & Max Pain
As expiry approaches, prices tend to be "pinned" near strikes with the highest open interest. This happens because market makers continuously delta-hedge as the price oscillates — their hedging activity dampens the very move they're hedging against, creating a magnetic pull toward the strike. Max pain is the strike where option buyers collectively lose the most at expiry — the level dealers profit most from. Historically, 0DTE expirations often settle within 0.25% of the max pain strike.
👤
Retail 0DTE Strategy
Retail traders use 0DTE primarily as directional momentum plays. A $100 0DTE call on SPY can return 500–1,000% if SPY moves up 0.5% by close — or expire worthless by 3 PM. Key principles: (1) trade with the flow direction, not against it; (2) size tiny — 0.5% per trade max; (3) enter before 10 AM when premium is cheap and exit before 2 PM when theta accelerates; (4) avoid news days without context. The high vol/OI ratios flagged in the Best Trades table indicate institutional conviction — following those strikes often yields the best risk-adjusted entries.
🏦
Institutional 0DTE Strategy
Institutions use 0DTE for four purposes: (1) intraday portfolio hedging — a fund long $500M SPY buys 0DTE puts to hedge an afternoon of event risk; (2) gamma scalping — market makers buy 0DTE ATM options and scalp delta as price moves; (3) pinning — large players short straddles at key strikes and defend them with stock to maximize premium collected; (4) momentum acceleration — directional funds buy large 0DTE call sweeps to both hedge their long stock and push the stock higher via forced dealer buying.
