Chart Types

Visual Guide to Dashboard Charts

Each chart type communicates a different layer of market intelligence. Here is what each one shows and how to read it.

📈 Candlestick Chart — Price Action

Each bar = one 4-hour session. Green body = closed higher than open (bullish). Red body = closed lower (bearish). Wicks show intraday high/low extremes. The ML model trains on 360 bars (60 days) of these candles.

🤖 ML Probability Gauge — Model Confidence

The arc fills 0–100% with the model confidence. Above 65% = strong BUY signal. Below 35% = strong SELL signal. 35–65% = HOLD. Color gradient shifts red → amber → green.

⚡ Gamma Exposure — Dealer Hedging Pressure

Green bars above zero = dealers long gamma (suppress vol, price stays in range). Red bars below zero = dealers short gamma (amplify moves). The tallest green bar = Gamma Wall. The zero crossing = Flip Level.

🌊 Option Flows — Tornado Chart

Each row = one strike price. Green bars right = call volume at that strike. Red bars left = put volume. Long bars = heavy institutional interest. Call-heavy upper strikes signal bullish positioning.

🌎 Market Breadth — Sentiment Gauge

The breadth bar shows the percentage of tracked assets with a BUY signal vs SELL. When >65% bullish, the market is broadly advancing. Below 35% bullish = broad weakness. This breadth reading confirms or challenges individual asset signals.

📊 Feature Importance — What Drives the Signal

Horizontal bars show each indicator's weight in the current signal. Longer bar = more influence. RSI, MACD, ATR, Bollinger Band width, and volume ratio are the top five features. Changes in feature importance reveal which market regime is currently dominant.

Concepts Explained

Key Dashboard Concepts

Each section of the dashboard uses a distinct analytical framework. Understanding these concepts unlocks the full power of the system.

🤖 Machine Learning Signal

An XGBoost gradient boosting model trained on 14 technical features from 4-hour OHLCV bars predicts whether the next bar will close higher or lower. The output is a probability (0–1) that determines the BUY/SELL/HOLD signal.

XGBoost BUY above 65% SELL below 35%

⚡ Gamma Exposure (GEX)

GEX quantifies how options market makers must adjust their stock hedges as price moves. Positive GEX zones suppress volatility and pin price. Negative GEX zones amplify moves. The Flip Level is the most important level — it separates low-vol from high-vol regimes.

Positive = Low Vol Negative = High Vol Flip Level = Key

🌊 Options Flow Analysis

Tracks real dollar premium flowing into calls vs puts across all strikes in real time. When large institutions buy calls aggressively (call sweeps), it often precedes a bullish price move. Net Dealer Delta tells you whether market makers are net buyers or sellers of the underlying stock.

Call Sweep = Bullish Put Sweep = Bearish Smart Money

⏳ 0DTE Options

Zero Days to Expiration options expire the same trading day. They have no time value remaining, so their price is almost entirely intrinsic value. Massive 0DTE volume in one direction can force market makers to hedge aggressively, creating self-fulfilling intraday price moves.

Highest Risk Intraday Only Theta = 0

Reference

Trading & ML Glossary

Definitions for every term used across the dashboard. Bookmark this section as a quick reference.

ATR — Average True Range
Measures the average price range over N periods. Used to set stop-loss distances and normalize position sizes across assets with different volatilities.
Backpropagation
The algorithm that trains neural networks by computing the gradient of the loss function with respect to each weight and updating weights to minimize prediction error.
Bollinger Bands
A volatility channel: a moving average ± 2 standard deviations. Price touching the upper band may signal overbought; lower band may signal oversold.
Call Option
A contract giving the buyer the right (not obligation) to buy 100 shares at the strike price before expiration. Buyers profit when the stock rises.
Call Sweep
A rapid, large purchase of call options spread across multiple exchanges simultaneously — a hallmark of institutional conviction buying.
Candlestick
A price bar showing open, high, low, and close. Green = bullish (close > open). Red = bearish (close < open). Wicks show intraday extremes.
Confluence
When multiple independent analytical methods (ML signal, GEX level, options flow, fundamentals) all point in the same direction — the highest-probability trade condition.
Delta
How much an option price moves per $1 move in the underlying. A delta of 0.5 means the option gains $0.50 for every $1 rise in the stock.
Flip Level
The strike price where dealer gamma crosses zero. Above = positive gamma (low vol). Below = negative gamma (high vol, accelerated moves). One of the most important GEX levels.
Forward P/E
Stock price divided by next year's projected earnings per share. Lower P/E relative to peers suggests undervaluation. Technology stocks typically trade at higher P/E multiples.
Gamma
The rate of change of delta as the underlying price moves. High gamma = option delta changes quickly. Options near expiration and at-the-money have the highest gamma.
Gamma Wall
The strike with the largest positive dealer gamma exposure. Acts as a magnetic attractor for price and a strong resistance level because dealers aggressively sell into rallies there.
GEX — Gamma Exposure
The aggregate gamma position held by options market makers across all strikes. Measures how much buying/selling dealers must do as price moves — the primary driver of intraday volatility regimes.
Gradient Boosting (XGBoost)
An ensemble ML algorithm that builds trees sequentially, each correcting the errors of the previous. The dominant method for tabular trading data due to its handling of non-linear relationships.
Iron Condor
An options strategy that profits from low volatility. Sell an out-of-the-money call and put, buy further out-of-the-money call and put. Maximum profit when price stays between the short strikes at expiration.
MACD
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. The difference between the 12-period and 26-period EMA, with a 9-period signal line. Crossovers identify momentum shifts and trend changes.
Market Breadth
The percentage of tracked assets with bullish ML signals vs. bearish. >65% bullish = broadly advancing market. Below 35% = broad weakness. Confirms or questions individual asset signals.
Max Pain
The options strike price at which the maximum dollar value of options expires worthless. Price gravitates toward max pain as expiration approaches because it minimizes total options payout.
Net Dealer Delta
The aggregate directional hedge requirement of all options market makers. Negative dealer delta = dealers must buy the underlying to hedge, creating persistent buying pressure.
Open Interest (OI)
The total number of options contracts that have been bought or sold but not yet settled. High OI at a strike indicates a concentration of institutional positioning that can act as support or resistance.
Put Option
A contract giving the buyer the right to sell 100 shares at the strike price before expiration. Buyers profit when the stock declines. Often used for portfolio hedging.
Put Wall
The strike with the heaviest put open interest. Acts as a strong support level because dealers must aggressively buy the underlying near this strike to hedge their put exposure.
Put-Call Ratio (PCR)
Total put open interest divided by total call open interest. PCR > 1.0 = more puts than calls = defensive/bearish positioning. Contrarians note extreme PCR readings as potential reversal signals.
RSI — Relative Strength Index
A momentum oscillator measuring speed of price changes on a 0–100 scale. Above 70 = overbought (potential reversal down). Below 30 = oversold (potential reversal up). Developed by J. Welles Wilder.
Sharpe Ratio
A strategy risk-adjusted return metric: (Return - Risk Free Rate) / Standard Deviation. A Sharpe above 1.0 indicates good risk-adjusted performance. Above 2.0 is excellent.
Strike Price
The fixed price at which an option contract can be exercised. Calls are profitable when the stock price exceeds the strike. Puts are profitable when the stock price falls below the strike.
Theta Decay
The daily reduction in an option's time value as expiration approaches. Accelerates dramatically in the final week. Sellers of options profit from theta; buyers are hurt by it.
0DTE (Zero Days to Expiration)
Options expiring the same trading day. No time premium remains — price is pure intrinsic value. Enormous leverage and risk. Institutional 0DTE flows can force large intraday price moves.
