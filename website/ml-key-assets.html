How to Read the Feature Heatmap
Each cell is green when the feature is bullish for that asset, red when bearish, and neutral otherwise. The Trend column is the overall ML verdict — a weighted combination of all 11 features plus the ensemble classifier's P(UP) probability. Click any column header to sort. Use the filter buttons to focus on specific groups or signals.
①RSI (14) — Relative Strength Index
Momentum oscillator measuring the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. Compares average gains vs average losses over 14 bars. The single highest-importance feature in the ML model (14.2%).
0–30 Oversold → Bullish reversal zone70–100 Overbought → Bearish reversal zone
②MACD Histogram
Difference between the MACD line (EMA12−EMA26) and the 9-bar signal line. Captures momentum shifts and divergences. Rising histogram = accelerating momentum. Second most important feature (11.8%).
Positive → Bullish momentumNegative → Bearish momentum
③ATR % — Average True Range
14-bar average true range expressed as a percentage of price. Measures current volatility. High ATR signals wider expected moves and larger stops are needed. Critical for position sizing and stop placement (10.7%).
<0.5% Low vol, tight stops>2% High vol, wide stops
④SMA20 Ratio — Price vs 20-bar SMA
Close price divided by the 20-bar simple moving average. Values above 1.0 mean price is above its short-term average (uptrend); below 1.0 means downtrend. Used to determine mean-reversion vs trend-following conditions (9.6%).
>1.002 Above SMA → Bullish<0.998 Below SMA → Bearish
⑤BB Position — Bollinger Band %B
Position of price within the Bollinger Bands (0 = lower band, 1 = upper band). Values near 0 indicate oversold conditions near support; values near 1 indicate overbought near resistance. Critical for mean-reversion timing (8.8%).
0.0–0.3 Near lower band → Oversold0.7–1.0 Near upper band → Overbought
⑥Volume Ratio
Current bar volume divided by the 20-bar rolling average volume. Values above 1.2 indicate elevated trading activity, confirming price moves. Volume spikes in the direction of trend add conviction to signals (8.2%).
>1.5× High conviction move<0.7× Low conviction, weak move
⑦5-Bar Return (%)
Percentage price change over the last 5 bars (5 hours on hourly charts, 5 days on daily). Captures short-term momentum and recent trend direction. Positive values indicate recent uptrend; negative = downtrend (7.4%).
Positive → Recent uptrendNegative → Recent downtrend
⑧OBV Trend — On-Balance Volume
Normalized 10-bar slope of On-Balance Volume (OBV), scaled to [−1, +1]. OBV adds volume on up bars and subtracts on down bars; rising OBV indicates institutional accumulation. Divergence from price is a leading indicator (6.3%).
>0.1 Buying pressure / accumulation<−0.1 Selling pressure / distribution
⑨Stochastic %K (14-period)
Position of current close within the 14-bar high-low range, expressed as 0–100%. Identifies overbought and oversold conditions similar to RSI but more sensitive to recent extremes. Used with RSI for confluence signals (5.7%).
0–20 Oversold zone80–100 Overbought zone
⑩ADX — Average Directional Index
Measures trend strength on a 0–100 scale. Does NOT indicate direction — it measures whether a trend exists. ADX >25 confirms a trending market where momentum strategies work; ADX <20 signals choppy/ranging conditions (4.9%).
<20 No trend / choppy>25 Strong trend present
⑪Sharpe Ratio (20-bar, annualized)
Rolling 20-bar Sharpe ratio annualized to a yearly scale: (avg daily return ÷ std dev) × √252. Measures risk-adjusted return — how much return is earned per unit of risk. Positive means the asset has been rewarding recently; negative means losses relative to volatility.
<0 Negative risk-adj return>1.0 Strong risk-adj return
