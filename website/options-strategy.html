Options Strategies Explained

📈 Long Call

Buy a call at or slightly above the current price. Best when strongly bullish in a low-IV environment where options are cheaper to buy. Risk is limited to the premium paid; reward is unlimited as the stock rises above the breakeven (strike + premium).

📉 Long Put

Buy a put at or slightly below the current price. Best when strongly bearish in a low-IV environment. Risk is limited to the premium paid; max profit is the strike minus premium if the stock falls toward zero.

🐂 Bull Put Spread

Sell an OTM put and buy a further OTM put for a net credit. Best when moderately bullish with elevated IV — collect premium while capping risk. Profit = credit received; max loss = spread width − credit.

🐻 Bear Call Spread

Sell an OTM call and buy a further OTM call for a net credit. Best when moderately bearish with elevated IV. Max profit if the stock stays below the short call strike; max loss = spread width − credit.

🦅 Iron Condor

Bear call spread + bull put spread = a net credit trade. Profit if the stock stays inside both short strikes at expiration. Best in a neutral, high-IV rangebound environment — time decay (theta) works in your favor.

🤖 ML Direction Signal

Direction is scored from 4 technical factors: RSI (momentum), MACD histogram (trend), SMA20 ratio (price vs. average), and Bollinger Band position. Score ≥ 2 → Bullish; ≤ −2 → Bearish; else Neutral.

📊 Vol Regime

ATR% compared to its 50-bar rolling median. Low vol (regime 0): buy options — they are cheap. Normal/High vol (regime 1–2): sell credit spreads — collect elevated premium that tends to mean-revert. IV crush after earnings is the classic example.

🎯 Probability of Profit

Derived from the short leg's Black-Scholes delta. For credit spreads: 1 − |Δ| ≈ probability the short option expires worthless (your profit). For debit strategies: Δ ≈ approximate probability of expiring in-the-money. These are risk-neutral model estimates.
