📊 Fundamental Score (0–100)

A composite score combining six factors: revenue growth (+15 max), earnings growth (+10 max), forward P/E valuation (±20), analyst consensus (±15 based on rating from Strong Buy to Strong Sell), net profitability (+5 if >15%), 52-week price momentum (±10), and upside to analyst mean target (±10). Baseline is 50. Scores above 65 indicate strong broad-based fundamentals.

💰 Valuation Metrics

Key ratios for comparing price to underlying fundamentals:

  • Forward P/E: Price relative to next 12-month earnings. <15 = value; >40 = growth premium.
  • Price/Book: Price vs. net asset value. High P/B implies market expects returns above book.
  • EV/EBITDA: Enterprise value vs. operating earnings. <10 often signals undervaluation; >25 is richly priced.
  • P/S: Useful for high-growth pre-profit companies where P/E is not meaningful.

📈 Growth & Profitability

Revenue and earnings growth rates drive long-term share price. Net Margin above 15% is exceptional; above 25% is elite-tier (typical of MSFT, NVDA, AAPL). ROE above 20% signals efficient use of shareholder capital. Strong growth at a reasonable P/E — measured by the PEG ratio — is the hallmark of compounders that outperform over decades.

🏛️ Financial Health

Balance sheet strength protects against downturns and enables opportunistic investment.

  • Current Ratio >1 means current assets cover current liabilities. <1 signals near-term liquidity risk.
  • Debt/Equity: Below 100% is conservative; above 200% warrants scrutiny of interest coverage.
  • Free Cash Flow: Cash generated after capital expenditure — the truest measure of a business's earning power and ability to fund dividends, buybacks, and M&A.

🎯 Analyst Consensus

Wall Street ratings are aggregated across all covering sell-side analysts. The mean recommendation scale runs: 1.0 = Strong Buy, 2.0 = Buy, 3.0 = Hold, 4.0 = Sell, 5.0 = Strong Sell. The price target range (low / mean / high) shows the spread of individual analyst expectations. A wide range signals high uncertainty; a narrow range signals strong conviction. More analysts covering a stock generally increases consensus reliability.

📅 Earnings Calendar

Earnings announcements are the single biggest recurring volatility driver for individual stocks. A beat + raise (beats EPS estimate and raises forward guidance) typically drives 5–15% moves up. A miss + lower can drop 10–20%. Options traders note that implied volatility spikes into earnings then collapses immediately after (“IV crush”) — making long options purchased just before earnings expensive. Analyst upgrades and downgrades following earnings also move prices significantly.
