📋 Options Expiration Impact (OpEx Dynamics)

Monthly OpEx effect: Every 3rd Friday, massive open interest expires. The week of OpEx typically sees pinning behavior near large-OI strikes. Post-OpEx week (week 4) often sees the largest directional move of the month as vanna/charm hedges unwind and dealers go gamma-flat.
Pre-OpEx (week 3)
Pinning to large open interest strikes. Gamma peaks. Vol compresses. Short-premium strategies outperform. Price gravitates to max pain by Thursday.
Post-OpEx (week 4)
Vanna/charm unwind drives directional move. Dealers go gamma-flat. New institutional positioning sets the tone for the following month. Often the cleanest trending week.

🔀 Long Gamma Regime

When dealers are net long gamma, they sell strength and buy weakness — creating a dampening, range-bound environment. Realized vol falls below implied vol. Mean-reversion strategies thrive.

  • Signal: Spot above Gamma Flip level
  • Strategy: Sell OTM premium, fade extremes, iron condors
  • Expected move: Contained within Gamma Wall and Put Wall
  • Risk: Sustained break above Gamma Wall triggers short squeeze

🔴 Short Gamma Regime

When dealers are net short gamma, they buy rallies and sell dips — amplifying every move. Realized vol exceeds implied vol. Momentum and breakout strategies thrive.

  • Signal: Spot below Gamma Flip level
  • Strategy: Trend-following, momentum, buy debit spreads
  • Expected move: Accelerating moves toward Put Wall
  • Risk: Put Wall provides mechanical floor from dealer buying

Institutional Delta Hedging & Dealer Flows
How option dealer hedging creates systematic non-fundamental price movements

🧠 How Dealer Delta Hedging Moves Markets

Options dealers (market makers) must remain delta-neutral on their books. When they sell a call option, they must buy the underlying to hedge. This continuous rebalancing is the largest systematic, non-fundamental force in modern equity markets — larger than CTAs, larger than most institutional flows.

The amplification math: A 1% move in SPY creates dealer delta changes worth billions. In a long-gamma regime, dealers re-hedge by selling into rallies and buying into dips. In short-gamma, they do the opposite — each 1% move forces additional hedging in the same direction, creating self-reinforcing momentum.
  • Vanna flow: When IV falls (post-event crush), dealer delta changes force large buying. Post-earnings/FOMC vol crush = systematic buying over 2-5 sessions
  • Charm flow: As 0DTE options approach expiry, deltas migrate to 0 or 1, forcing dealers to unwind hedges. Peak effect: final 90 minutes of 0DTE day
  • Speed flow: Rate of gamma decay — high speed means dealer GEX hedging will drop rapidly, potentially breaking a range-bound regime

Bull Triggers to Watch

  • ML consensus > 60% bullish — broad model agreement across sectors
  • VIX compression below 18 — vanna flows force systematic dealer buying as IV drops

Base Case Assumptions

  • SPY oscillates around Gamma Flip — mixed long/short gamma days, range-bound
  • ML consensus 45-55% bullish — no strong directional signal, sector rotation
  • VIX 16-22 — normal volatility environment, balanced hedging
  • Balanced call/put premium — institutional hedging offset by speculative calls
  • Earnings risk contained — individual stock vol without index contagion

Bear Risks to Monitor

  • Break below Gamma Flip — triggers short-gamma cascade: dealers sell dips, CTAs flip short, vol spikes
  • ML consensus < 35% bullish — broad model agreement on downside
  • VIX spike above 25 — risk-parity and vol-targeting funds forced to sell, amplifying drawdown
  • Put premium surge — institutional panic hedging signals large player concern
  • Macro event shock — FOMC surprise, geopolitical, credit event breaking correlation assumptions
